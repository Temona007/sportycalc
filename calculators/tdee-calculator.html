What is Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)?

TDEE represents your overall daily calorie burn, factoring in not just your resting metabolism (BMR) but also physical activity, exercise, and even the thermic effect of food. It's calculated by multiplying your BMR by an activity multiplier based on your lifestyle—from sedentary to highly active. This gives a more complete picture than BMR alone, allowing you to set precise goals like a calorie deficit for fat loss or a surplus for gains.

Our calculator builds on the reliable Mifflin-St Jeor equation for BMR, then applies standard activity factors endorsed by health organizations like the World Health Organization (WHO). This approach ensures accuracy for everyday users, from beginners to fitness pros.

Common Questions About TDEE

How does TDEE differ from BMR?

BMR is your resting burn; TDEE adds daily movement and activities.

Should I recalculate often?

Yes, every 4-6 weeks or after lifestyle changes like starting a new workout routine.

Is TDEE accurate for everyone?

It's a strong estimate, but factors like muscle mass or hormones can vary—use it as a starting point.
