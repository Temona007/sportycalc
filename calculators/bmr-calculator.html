What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?

Basal Metabolic Rate represents the minimum number of calories your body requires to perform essential functions like breathing, circulating blood, and regulating temperature while at complete rest. It's influenced by factors such as age, gender, height, weight, and body composition. Unlike other metrics, BMR focuses solely on your resting energy expenditure, providing a foundation for calculating your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).

Our calculator uses the scientifically validated Mifflin-St Jeor equation, widely regarded as one of the most accurate formulas for estimating BMR. Backed by research from the American Dietetic Association, this method outperforms older equations like Harris-Benedict for modern populations.

Common Questions About BMR

Is BMR the same as RMR?

Resting Metabolic Rate (RMR) is similar but includes slight activity; BMR is strictly at rest.

How often should I recalculate?

Update every few months or after significant weight changes.

Can BMR change?

Yes, with age, muscle building, or hormonal shifts—recalculate as needed.
