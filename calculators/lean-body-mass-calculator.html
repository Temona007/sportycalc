What is Lean Body Mass (LBM)?

Lean Body Mass represents your total body weight minus body fat, providing insights into your muscular and skeletal structure. Unlike total weight, LBM highlights composition changes from workouts or diets. We use proven equations like:

  • Boer Formula: For men: LBM = (0.407 × weight in kg) + (0.267 × height in cm) − 19.2; For women: LBM = (0.252 × weight) + (0.473 × height) − 48.3.
  • James Formula: For men: LBM = (1.1 × weight) − 128 × (weight² / height²); For women: LBM = (1.07 × weight) − 148 × (weight² / height²).

Backed by fitness research, these methods offer quick estimates without advanced scans, complementing tools like DEXA for home use.

FAQ

How does LBM differ from BMI?

LBM focuses on fat-free mass; BMI is overall weight-to-height. LBM is better for athletes who carry more muscle.

Is it accurate for everyone?

Estimates are solid but vary by body type; recalculate after significant weight or composition changes.

Can women use it?

Yes, formulas adjust for gender—women typically have lower LBM due to body composition.

Why track LBM?

It guides protein needs and helps prevent muscle loss during calorie deficits.
