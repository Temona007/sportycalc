What is Daily Protein Intake?

Protein is an essential macronutrient that builds and repairs tissues, supports immune function, and aids in hormone production. Your daily needs vary by factors like weight (in kg or lbs), lifestyle (sedentary to athlete), and objectives—higher for muscle building or during calorie deficits to preserve lean mass. Guidelines from sources like the International Society of Sports Nutrition (ISSN) recommend 1.2–2.2 grams per kg of body weight, depending on activity.

Our calculator uses evidence-based ranges: sedentary (1.2–1.6 g/kg), active (1.4–1.8 g/kg), muscle building (1.6–2.0 g/kg), and athletes (1.8–2.2 g/kg). It also factors in meals per day for practical per-meal breakdowns, making it easy to hit targets with whole foods or supplements.

FAQ

How much protein for fat loss?

Aim for 1.6–2.2 g/kg to preserve muscle—higher in deficits.

Does activity level matter?

Yes; sedentary folks need less than athletes to avoid excess calories.

Can I split protein over meals?

Absolutely—our calculator suggests even distribution for better absorption (20–40g per meal).

What are good protein sources?

Lean meats, eggs, dairy, beans, or supplements—focus on variety for complete amino acids.
