What is One Rep Max (1RM)?

One Rep Max refers to the heaviest weight you can lift for exactly one repetition with proper form. It's a key benchmark in strength sports like powerlifting or CrossFit, used to set training percentages for programs (e.g., 80% of 1RM for hypertrophy). Direct 1RM testing can be risky, so calculators provide safe estimates using sub-maximal lifts.

Our calculator supports multiple proven formulas:

  • Brzycki (most common): Ideal for general use, especially with 1–10 reps.
  • Epley: Great for higher reps, emphasizing endurance.
  • Lander: Accurate for varied rep ranges.
  • Average of All: A balanced estimate combining formulas for reliability.

Backed by exercise science from sources like the National Strength and Conditioning Association (NSCA), these methods use equations like Brzycki's: 1RM = weight × (36 / (37 − reps)).

Why Calculate Your 1RM?

  • Smart Programming: Base workouts on percentages—e.g., 85% for power, 65% for endurance—to avoid plateaus.
  • Progress Tracking: Monitor strength improvements over time without maxing out every session.
  • Injury Prevention: Estimate safely instead of pushing limits, especially for beginners or during deloads.
  • Free and Secure: Quick results with no data sharing—perfect for gym apps or mobile use.

FAQ

How accurate are 1RM estimates?

Very reliable for 1–10 reps; less so for higher rep ranges. Test periodically for validation.

Which formula is best?

Brzycki works well for most people; try Average if you're unsure or want a balanced estimate.

Can I use it for any exercise?

Yes—bench press, squat, deadlift, and other compound lifts. Form matters for real-world application.
