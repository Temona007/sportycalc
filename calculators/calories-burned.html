What is Calories Burned?

Calories burned refer to the energy your body uses during physical activities, measured in kcal (kilocalories). Factors like weight, intensity, duration, and type of exercise influence this—e.g., running burns more than walking at the same pace. For distance-based activities (walking, running, cycling), we use MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) values from compendiums like the one by the American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM). This provides accurate estimates to help offset daily calorie consumption and achieve goals like weight loss or endurance building.

Use this to estimate calories burned each day, complementing tools like TDEE for a full picture of energy balance.

Why Calculate Calories Burned?

  • Activity Tracking: Quantify workouts to motivate consistency and avoid underestimating effort.
  • Weight Management: Balance burned calories against intake for deficits (loss) or surpluses (gains).
  • Personalized Fitness: Adjust routines—e.g., see how cycling distance stacks up to running time.
  • Free and Private: No apps required; all calculations happen in your browser for secure, quick insights.

Common Questions About Calories Burned

Duration vs. distance—which is better?
Use distance for pace-based activities like running; duration for timed ones like yoga.
How accurate are estimates?
Solid for general use, based on METs—but individual metabolism varies; wearables can refine.
Does it include daily activities?
Yes, add non-exercise like housework to estimate total daily burn.
Update for weight changes?
Yes, recalculate as weight affects burn rates.
