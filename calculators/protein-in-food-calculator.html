What is Protein Content in Foods?

Protein is a vital macronutrient for muscle repair, hormone production, and overall health, with sources varying in grams per serving. This calculator uses average values from USDA databases and nutritional research (e.g., chicken breast: ~31g per 100g). It covers popular foods like chicken, beef, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, and grains, converting units (grams or ounces) for accuracy. Note: Actual content can vary by preparation or brand—use as an estimate.

Common Questions About Protein in Foods

How accurate are the estimates?

Based on averages; check labels for exacts, as cooking methods (e.g., grilled vs. fried) can affect values.

Why vary by food type?

Animal sources like Turkey breast are denser (~25g/100g), while plants like Chickpeas (cooked) offer ~9g/100g but with fiber bonuses.

Can I add custom foods?

Not yet, but our list covers essentials—suggest more in comments!

Does it include cooked vs. raw?

Defaults to common forms (e.g., cooked for lentils); adjust amounts accordingly.
