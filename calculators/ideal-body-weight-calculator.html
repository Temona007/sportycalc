What is Ideal Body Weight?

Ideal Body Weight represents a theoretical "perfect" weight based on height, assuming average build and minimal body fat. The primary Devine formula (developed for clinical use) starts at 50kg (men) or 45.5kg (women) for 152cm, adding 2.3kg per inch over. Adjustments like Peterson or others refine for accuracy. Backed by medical resources, it's handy for scenarios like anesthesia or nutrition planning, but remember—it's an estimate, not a one-size-fits-all.

Our calculator incorporates these methods for reliable results, emphasizing practicality over rigidity.

Common Questions About Ideal Body Weight

Does it account for muscle?

Basic formulas don't—athletes may exceed IBW healthily; use body fat tools for context.

How does it differ from healthy weight?

IBW is a single number; healthy is a range—explore our Healthy Weight Calculator for broader views.

When to use adjustments?

For obesity or extremes, adjusted IBW modifies for actual weight—ideal for clinical settings.
