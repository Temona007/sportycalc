What is a Healthy Weight?

A healthy weight is typically a range where your Body Mass Index (BMI) falls between 18.5 and 24.9, but it accounts for more than just numbers—factors like muscle mass, bone density, and age play in. Formulas like Devine, Robinson, Miller, and Hamwi (from reliable sources) provide varied estimates, averaging for a practical band. This isn't about perfection; it's about promoting energy, mobility, and longevity, as endorsed by health bodies like the World Health Organization (WHO).

Common Questions About Healthy Weight

How does age affect it?

Older adults may have slightly higher healthy ranges due to muscle changes—formulas adjust accordingly.

Is it the same as ideal weight?

No; healthy is a broader range, while ideal is more specific—our calculator shows both formula-based estimates and the WHO healthy BMI band for comparison.

What if I'm athletic?

Ranges might underestimate muscle; consult pros for body comp analysis.
