What is Body Fat Percentage?

Body fat percentage measures the proportion of fat mass to your total body weight, providing insights into fitness levels, health risks, and muscle-to-fat ratios. Unlike BMI, which doesn't differentiate between fat and muscle, body fat calculations offer a more accurate view of your physique. Essential fat supports vital functions, while excess can lead to issues like heart disease—aiming for healthy ranges (e.g., 14–24% for women, 6–17% for men) promotes overall wellness.

Our calculator employs the U.S. Navy Circumference Method, a research-backed formula developed by the Department of Defense. It uses neck, waist, and hip measurements (for women) or height and waist (for men) to deliver quick, non-invasive estimates, validated against more advanced techniques like DEXA scans.

How to Use Our Body Fat Calculator

  2. Input Data: Enter your gender and measurements in cm or inches.

Common Questions About Body Fat

  • How accurate is the Navy method? It's about 3–4% off from lab tests for most people—great for home use but not medical-grade.
  • What affects body fat? Age, gender, genetics, diet, and exercise; recalculate monthly for updates.
  • Is low body fat always better? No—essential fat is crucial; consult pros if aiming below healthy levels.
