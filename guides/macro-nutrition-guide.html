Macro Nutrition Guide

Understanding protein, carbohydrates, and fats — and how to balance them for your fitness goals.

If you want to lose fat, build muscle, or improve performance, understanding macronutrients (macros) is essential. Calories matter — but where those calories come from plays a major role in body composition, recovery, and overall health.

This guide explains what macros are, how much you need, and how to set the right macro split for your goals.

What Are Macros?

Macronutrients are the three main nutrient groups your body needs in large amounts:

  • Protein – 4 calories per gram
  • Carbohydrates – 4 calories per gram
  • Fat – 9 calories per gram

Each macro provides energy, but they also have unique roles in the body. Balancing these correctly helps optimize fat loss, muscle growth, athletic performance, and hormone health.

Protein: The Muscle Builder

Protein is essential for:

  • Muscle repair and growth
  • Preserving lean mass during fat loss
  • Satiety (helps you feel full longer)
  • Supporting metabolism

How Much Protein Do You Need?

For most active individuals:

  • 1.6–2.2 g per kg of body weight
    (0.7–1.0 g per lb)

Higher intake is especially beneficial when building muscle, losing fat, or training intensely. Use our protein intake calculator for personalized targets.

Adequate protein is the foundation of any effective macro plan.

Carbohydrates: Your Primary Fuel Source

Carbohydrates are your body's preferred energy source, especially for:

  • Strength training
  • Cardio workouts
  • High-intensity exercise
  • Daily movement (NEAT)

Higher carb intake generally suits active individuals, athletes, and people training 3–6+ times per week. Lower carb approaches can work for some individuals, particularly in sedentary lifestyles or specific dietary preferences.

Carbs support performance — and performance supports results.

Fat: Essential for Hormones & Health

Dietary fat is crucial for hormones, fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K), brain function, and energy.

Minimum Fat Intake

Avoid going too low. A good minimum range: 0.5–1 g per kg of body weight. Extremely low-fat diets can negatively affect hormones and overall well-being.

How to Set Your Macro Split

The smartest way to set macros is step-by-step:

  1. Step 1: Calculate Your Calories — First, determine your daily calorie target (based on your TDEE and goal). Use our calorie calculator for targets.
  2. Step 2: Set Protein — Prioritize protein first (1.6–2.2 g/kg).
  3. Step 3: Set Minimum Fat — Set fat at 0.5–1 g/kg.
  4. Step 4: Fill Remaining Calories with Carbs — After protein and fat are set, allocate the remaining calories to carbohydrates.

Example Strategy

  • Cutting (fat loss): High protein, moderate fat, moderate carbs
  • Lean bulking (muscle gain): High protein, moderate fat, higher carbs
  • Keto / Low-carb: High fat, moderate protein, very low carbs

A macro calculator can automate this process and personalize your targets instantly.

Important Notes About Macros

  • Total calories still determine weight change.
  • Macros influence body composition and performance.
  • Track progress for 2–4 weeks and adjust as needed.
  • Consistency matters more than perfection.
  • There is no single "perfect" macro ratio — the best split is the one that fits your lifestyle and training level.

Why Tracking Macros Works

Macro tracking gives you:

  • Better control over fat loss
  • Improved muscle retention
  • Performance optimization
  • Nutritional awareness
